  1. Lead with the gap. A title works when it opens a question the viewer can't leave unanswered.
  2. Front-load the payoff. The hook needs to land in the first 3-4 words — feeds truncate the rest.
  3. Numbers and specifics beat vague claims. "7 mistakes" beats "common mistakes" every time.
  4. Thumbnail text is not the title. Keep it to 2-4 words, it's a visual flag, not a sentence.
  5. Hashtags should mix broad and niche. A couple of huge tags for reach, a few specific ones for the right audience.
  6. The description's first line matters most. It shows in search and previews before anyone clicks "more."
